Kodokushi, or kodokushi (English: Kodokushi, Japanese: 孤独死; "lonely death" in translation) is a Japanese phenomenon describing death alone, when people die at home, forgotten by everyone: most often used when a person died at home due to illness or accident and no one noticed his death.

In essence, kodokushi is a silhouette of a human body, formed as a result of fluids flowing out of a corpse during its decomposition and imprinted where death caught the unfortunate, a kind of imprint of a lonely dead man ...

The kodokushi phenomenon was first described in the 1980s. In 2008, more than 2,000 bodies were found in Tokyo, lying lifeless in their apartments for several months.
